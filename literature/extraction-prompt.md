# Paper Extraction Prompt

Use this prompt when processing each paper. Copy, fill in the paper details, 
and paste to Claude along with the PDF.

---

## Prompt Template

```
This is a paper for a literature review supporting a preliminary study of 
balance-focused training applied to golf swing weight transfer.

The study will measure changes in centre of pressure timing and magnitude before 
and after a training period.

Please extract the following from the attached paper:

**Citation**: Full citation  
**DOI**:  
**Study type**: RCT / Observational / Cross-sectional / Review / Meta-analysis / Case study  
**Population**: n, sport/skill level, age range, sex  
**Intervention**: Describe exactly what was done  
**Comparator**: Control condition if any  
**Duration**: Training period length and frequency  
**Outcome measures**: List every outcome measure used and how it was measured  
**Key findings**: Main results in plain language, include effect sizes if reported  
**Statistical approach**: What analysis was used  
**Relevance to trial design**: Specifically what this tells us about:
  - Appropriate training duration
  - Appropriate outcome measures
  - Expected effect sizes
  - Likely confounders
**Limitations noted**: What the authors acknowledge  
**Quality assessment**: High / Medium / Low and brief reason  
**Direct quotes worth keeping**: Any specific sentences worth quoting verbatim  

Flag anything that directly contradicts assumptions in our experiment design.
```

## Priority Papers (extract first)

1. **TOGU training golf 4 weeks** (PMC11587031)  
   Most comparable design — balance training, golf, before/after, neuromuscular outcomes

2. **GRF systematic review** (Sports Medicine, DOI: 10.1007/s40279-025-02391-3)  
   Defines what GRF outcome measures are standard and what natural variability looks like

3. **Foot CoP intervention golf** (Roehampton / J Sports Sci 2019, DOI: 10.1080/02640414.2019.1614714)  
   Closest methodological precedent — manipulates foot pressure, measures lower body kinematics

4. **Ground-X-Factor preprint** (Preprints.org 2024)  
   Most recent foot proprioception intervention in golf — immediate effects on GRF and club speed

5. **Proprioceptive training sports performance systematic review** (PMC11225257)  
   Outcome measures across sports, effect sizes, training durations
